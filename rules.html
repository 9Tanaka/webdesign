rules

1. Community Behavior

  • Be respectful to all members and Vtubers.
  • No harassment, bullying, hate speech, or toxic behavior.
  • Do not spam, post repeated content, or advertise unrelated products/services.

2. What Should Not Be on This Site

2.1 Speculation & Rumor

  • Avoid unverified claims about future events, personal lives, or internal matters that are not publicly confirmed.
  • If including speculative content, clearly label it and separate it from factual content.

2.2 Personal Opinions (on main pages)

Core information pages should maintain a neutral point of view. Do not use "I think / she is the best / fans believe that…" on pages meant for facts.

Opinions, analysis, or "fan takes" should reside in user pages, discussion pages, or specifically designated "opinions / reviews" sections.

2.3 NSFW / Explicit Content

  • Explicit sexual content, graphic violence, or content inappropriate for general audiences must not be uploaded or displayed.
  • If artwork involves borderline themes, the uploader must clearly mark it and follow stricter review.
  • No erotic NSFW content is allowed unless the site explicitly states it supports an adult section (and that is out of scope for this project, so better to disallow).

3. Fan Content, User Contributions & Uploads

  • Fanart, memes, clips, etc. are welcome only if properly credited to the original creator (when known).
  • Users must confirm they have permission / right to use content they upload (or that the content is in public domain).
  • Altered work or derivative content must also respect copyright and credits.
  • No mass-posting or flooding with low-effort content (e.g. one-liner memes without substance).

4. User Pages, Talk Pages, & Signatures

  • Users may post opinions, personal content, or casual remarks in user profile pages, user blogs, or talk pages—these are more flexible.
  • Do not edit or alter other users' profile pages or talk pages without consent (except to correct formatting or remove content violating rules).
  • Always sign your messages on talk pages (e.g. with "~~~~" in a wiki environment).

5. Vandalism, Spam & Bad Edits

  • Intentional vandalism (removing content, inserting nonsense, defacing pages) is not tolerated.
  • Spam, repeated changes with no meaningful content, or "badge boosting" via low-value edits are discouraged.
  • Editors should use the "Undo" function or revert to earlier good versions when fixing vandalism; if unsure, report to admins/moderators.

6. Code of Conduct & Community Behavior

  • Treat all users and Vtubers with respect, regardless of fandom.
  • No harassment, hate speech, doxxing, slurs, or discrimination (race, gender, sexuality, religion, etc.).
  • Avoid flame wars, trolling, or targeting individuals for attacks.
  • Keep political, sensitive real-world debates out of general pages unless directly relevant and handled neutrally.

7. Style, Formatting & Neutrality

  • Use consistent style guidelines (e.g. capitalization, date format, transliteration rules).
  • Use citations / references for factual claims (e.g. links to official sources, announcements).
  • Avoid promotional language (e.g. "This Vtuber is the best") on main pages.
  • Maintain neutrality especially in profiles, agency pages, ranking, or event descriptions.

8. Enforcement & Penalties

  • First offense: Warning explaining which rule was violated and how to fix.
  • Repeated minor offenses: Temporary suspension (e.g. 1 day, 7 days).
  • Continued or severe violations: Long-term ban / permanent ban.
  • Admins/moderators may skip directly to stronger penalties if the violation is major (doxxing, illegal content, harassment).
  • Users who attempt to evade bans by creating new accounts (sockpuppets) may face permanent restrictions.

9. Appeals & Moderation Transparency

  • Moderation decisions should come with clear explanations to the affected user.
  • Users may appeal bans/warnings via support / moderator message (depending on site infrastructure).
  • Mod actions (edits, deletions, bans) should ideally be logged or traceable, to maintain accountability.

10. Revision & Updates

  • These rules may be updated over time. Users should adhere to the latest version.
  • Substantial changes to rules should be announced / highlighted in a visible place (e.g. front page, news).
  • Older versions may remain archived for reference, but users are bound to follow the current rules.
